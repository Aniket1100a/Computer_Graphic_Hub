Introduction to Polygons

Understanding Polygons in Computer Graphics

Polygons are fundamental geometric shapes widely used in computer graphics. They are closed figures with straight sides. Polygons play a vital role in various applications, including computer-aided design, video games, and simulations.

Properties of Polygons:

  • Polygons are made up of line segments.
  • Each segment intersects exactly two others, with one common endpoint.
  • Polygon sides cannot cross each other.

Types of Polygons:

Polygons can be classified based on the arrangement of their sides:

  • Convex Polygons: All interior angles are less than 180 degrees.
  • Concave Polygons: At least one interior angle is greater than 180 degrees.
  • Complex Polygons: Polygons that are neither convex nor concave.

Applications of Polygons:

Understanding polygons is crucial for:

  • Creating 3D models in computer graphics software.
  • Defining the boundaries of objects in simulations.
  • Rendering complex shapes in video games.
