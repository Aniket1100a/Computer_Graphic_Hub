Understanding Pixels in Computer Graphics

What Are Pixels?

A pixel, short for "picture element," is the smallest unit of a digital image or display screen. It represents a single point in an image and contains information about its color, brightness, and position.

Pixel Resolution

Pixel resolution refers to the number of pixels in an image or on a display screen. Higher resolutions result in sharper and more detailed images.

Color Representation

Pixels can represent colors using various color models, such as RGB (Red, Green, Blue) or CMYK (Cyan, Magenta, Yellow, Key/Black).

Pixel Density

Pixel density, often measured in pixels per inch (PPI) or dots per inch (DPI), determines the quality of an image or display. Higher pixel density results in smoother and clearer visuals.

Pixel Art

Pixel art is a unique form of digital art that embraces the pixel's simplicity and limited color palette to create charming and nostalgic images and animations.
