Course Objectives and Outcomes

Welcome to the "Course Objectives and Outcomes" page of Computer Graphics Hub, where we outline the guiding principles and expectations of our Computer Graphics course. Our mission is to empower students with the knowledge and skills required for the design, development, and testing of modeling, rendering, and animation solutions in diverse fields, including entertainment, sciences, and engineering.

Course Objectives:

  1. Remembering: In the initial phase of the course, we introduce learners to the fundamental concepts of Computer Graphics.
  2. Understanding: As the course progresses, students delve deeper into the various algorithms that underpin the generation and rendering of graphical figures.
  3. Applying: Mathematics plays a crucial role in computer graphics, and students become well-acquainted with the mathematical foundations behind graphical transformations.
  4. Understanding: We explore a broad spectrum of methods and techniques related to projections, animation, shading, illumination, and lighting.
  5. Creating: Our course empowers students to create interactive graphics using OpenGL, fostering their creativity and technical proficiency.

Course Outcomes:

  1. Identify and understand the basic terminologies of Computer Graphics and interpret the mathematical foundation of key concepts.
  2. Apply mathematical principles to develop computer programs for elementary graphic operations, laying a strong foundation for practical applications.
  3. Illustrate their mastery of windowing and clipping concepts, applying various algorithms effectively to fill and clip polygons.
  4. Grasp and apply core concepts of computer graphics, including transformation in two and three dimensions, viewing, and projection.
  5. Gain insight into color models, lighting, shading models, and hidden surface elimination, ensuring a comprehensive understanding of rendering techniques.
  6. Cultivate the ability to create engaging and effective programs utilizing advanced concepts such as curves, fractals, animation, and gaming.
